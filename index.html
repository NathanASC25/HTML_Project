Modern Anti-Tank Ammunition

Inspired by War Thunder

By Nathan Chin

There are many types of anti-tank ammunition that have been developed throughout history, including but not limited to:

  • APBC
  • HEAT-FS
  • APCBC
  • APDS
  • APCR or HVAP

However, I will only display information about the APFSDS and ATGM rounds in this document.


APFSDS (Kinetic Round)

APFSDS, which stands for Armor Piercing Fin Stabilized Discarding Sabot, is a type of anti-tank ammunition
that is used to defeat modern tank defenses through high velocity and pinpoint surface area.
The design is simiar to that of an arrow. It relies on brute kinetic force rather than chemically
charged reactions to defeat enemy armor. As of now, militaries around the world have been utilizing this
technology for more than 50 years! Below are images of APFSDS.

ATGM (Chemical Round)


ATGM, which stands for Anti-Tank Guided Missile, is a type of anti-tank ammunition that is used to defeat modern
enemy defenses by chemically-charged means. When the projectile detects obstruction, it detonates and fires a jet
of high-velocity, molten metal to penetrate heavy armor. Light armored vehicles are also vulnerable to this particular
weapon's explosive force. The ATGM has been developed since the 1950s and 1960s! Below are images of the ATGM.
